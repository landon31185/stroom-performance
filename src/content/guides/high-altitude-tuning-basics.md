  - name: "Read the day's DA"
    text: "Density altitude combines air pressure, temperature, and humidity into one number. Higher DA = thinner air = less oxygen to burn."
  - name: "Match fuel to air"
    text: "Thinner air needs less fuel to hit the same ratio — but you still need the delivery capacity when you dial boost back up. Log fuel pressure through the run."
  - name: "Manage the boost curve"
    text: "At high DA you often lean on boost to recover power. Shape the curve so you don't overwhelm traction on an un-prepped surface."
  - name: "Confirm converter behavior"
    text: "Stall shifts with air density. Verify the converter is flashing where you expect at your actual DA, not where a sea-level spec sheet promised."

Most tuning advice assumes sea level. Race at 6,600 ft and the rules change:
the engine is fighting hypoxia, the converter behaves differently, and the fuel
math moves. This is the expertise Stroom is built on — the parts we sell are the
parts that survive these conditions.
